University of Singapore

History

The National University of Singapore is well-known for its high impact research activities in life sciences and biomedicine, engineering, social sciences, and natural sciences. Year of estabishment - 1905

Activities

Art Club

The Art Club is a place for practicing artists to hone in on their skills, develop their techniques and portfolios, collaborate with other artists like themselves, create bonds with the community through the arts, and learn how to work together through group projects that will beautify the school and community.

Badminton Club

Badminton is a game played by two or four players on a rectangular court with a high net across the middle. The players try to score points by hitting a small object called a shuttlecock across the net using a racket.

Aquaculture club

Aquaculture is breeding, raising, and harvesting fish, shellfish, and aquatic plants. Basically, it's farming in water. U.S. aquaculture is an environmentally responsible source of food and commercial products, helps to create healthier habitats, and is used to rebuild stocks of threatened or endangered species.

Top classes (Courses)

MBA/ Business/ Management

MBA Business Management is a specialization of MBA courses that focuses on making skilled individuals who know how to operate businesses, manage crises, offer solutions with limited resources and demonstrate discipline, development and cross-cultural leadership.

IT/ Computing

Information technology (IT) is the use of any computers, storage, networking and other physical devices, infrastructure and processes to create, process, store, secure and exchange all forms of electronic data.

Engineering

Engineers design, build, and maintain various complex systems and structures, from buildings to software. While there are many engineering professions, there is one consistent engineering mindset: to innovate.
